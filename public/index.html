Welcome to Cryptid

Sea-serpents, yetis, the chupacabra: you’ve studied the footage, connected the dots, and gathered what meagre evidence you could. You’re close – soon the whole world will know the truth behind the cryptid.

You have come together with a group of like-minded cryptozoologists to finally uncover the elusive creature, but the glory of discovery is too rich a prize to share. Without giving away some of what you know, you will never succeed in locating the beast, but reveal too much and your name will be soon forgotten!

Play

Overview

You are all cryptozoologists, trying to be the first to discover definitive proof of a cryptid in the wilds of North America. Each player will be given a unique clue – one piece of crucial information about where the creature lives. When combined, the clues identify a single space on the map – the creature’s habitat. Each player’s clue either states an area where the creature can be found, or where it cannot be found, based on the terrain and structures on the board. During the game, you will ask each other questions with the aim of guessing each other’s clues. The first player to correctly use all of the clues to find the habitat wins the game.

How To Play

Cryptid is a deduction board game where players search for a hidden creature. Players take turns asking questions, making searches, or attempting to solve the mystery. The game ends when someone correctly identifies the cryptid's location. For detailed rules, check out the Cryptid rulebook. It's a thrilling game of deduction and strategy that keeps players engaged until the very end!

About

Cryptid is a strategic board game designed by Hal Duncan and Ruth Veevers, first published in 2018 by Osprey Games. Set in the world of cryptozoology, players take on the roles of cryptozoologists searching for an elusive creature in the wilderness. The game revolves around deduction, logic, and strategic placement of pieces on the game board.

Each player receives a clue about the cryptid's habitat, such as its preferred terrain or climate. Through a series of questions and deductions, players narrow down the possible locations of the cryptid on the modular game board, which represents different types of terrain like forests, swamps, and mountains. Players must carefully analyze their opponents' actions and use logic to determine the cryptid's location while concealing their own.

Cryptid is known for its tense gameplay and requires players to think critically and strategically to outwit their opponents. The game typically accommodates 3 to 5 players and offers various levels of complexity, making it accessible to both casual and seasoned gamers. It has gained praise for its unique theme, engaging mechanics, and high replay value.
